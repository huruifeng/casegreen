About

1. Introduction

CaseGreen.info is a non-profit project providing reference statistics of US visa processing for TOTALLY FREE.
CaseGreen.info makes data-driven analysis to result in a reliable prediction of your visa application journey.
We dedicated to track and predict case status as good as possible, based on case processing data from USCIS and user inputs, but the results are not guaranteed.

We would like to build an information sharing community for everyone, especially for all the visa applicants. Everyone can access the website, query data, and share information here. This will be a FREE land for all the users.

Meanwhile, all the supports for running this project will be coming from you. Donations are the only source of income to keep this project running. If you like, you may contribute your generosity to make this project better and better!

3. FAQ

(1) Who are we ?
We are a group of immigrants, and experienced the whole processes of several visa applications. We are truly and definitely understanding the worries, anxieties, and uncertain exceptions during the painful, also maybe delightful, journey of preparing forms/documents, submitting applications and waiting for the notices. Here we are leading this project to provide as more information/data/statistics as we could to relieve your anxieties.
Immigrants help future immigrants.

(2) What are the data sources ?
The original data are from the USCIS (Websites, Emma, USCIS APIs,et.al.) and also the user corrections. The data is updated daily,usually at early morning.

(3) How to read the Receipt Number ?
The USCIS Receipt notice generated is sent to applicants as ‘Form I-797C, Notice of Action‘. The top of the receipt notice indicates this form number and details. The I-797C form generated has an indication on it that says ‘Notice Type‘ as ‘Receipt Notice‘, which confirms that it is a USCIS Receipt Notice.
The Receipt notice also has details about the application or case type, the received date, receipt number, applicant name, and other important information. The applicant or attorney, who files the application with USCIS gets the physical copy of the receipt notice mailed to them.

USCIS receipt number that is generated by the USCIS online case management system is based on a set of criteria to indicate where it is being processed, when it was filed, etc. It is a 13-digit number. The receipt number always starts with 3 alphabets like LIN, MSC, EAC, WAC, etc. It is followed by a 10-digit number.
Center list:

  • EAC – Eastern Adjudication Center (now known as Vermont Service Center)
  • IOE – USCIS online account (efile)
  • LIN – Lincoln Service Center (now known as Nebraska Service Center)
  • MSC – Missouri Service Center (now known as National Benefits Center)
  • SRC – Southern Regional Center (now known as Texas Service Center)
  • WAC – Western Adjudication Center (now known as California Service Center)
  • YSC – Potomac Service Center
USCIS Receipt Number has two main formats. The format depends on the filing location of the application or petition with USCIS as listed below :
  • (i). USCIS Service Centers - (Denote as SC in our website)
    • Receipt Number Format : XXX-XX-XXX-5-XXXX
    • Example : EAC-21-123-5-0345 (This is: Center-Year-Day-Code-Series)
  • (ii). USCIS Lockbox Location - (Denote as LB in our website)
    • Receipt Number Format : XXX-XX-9-XXX-XXXX
    • Example : MSC-22-9-001-2345 (This is: Center-Year-Code-Series-SubSeries)

(4) How is the Received Date determined ?
The Received Date is defined as the action taken date of the following status:

  • Fees Were Waived
  • Card Was Received By USCIS Along With My Letter
  • Case Accepted By The USCIS Lockbox
  • Case Was Received
  • Case Was Received and A Receipt Notice Was Sent
  • Case Was Received At Another USCIS Office
  • Document and Letter Was Received
  • Document And Letter Was Received And Under Review
  • Fingerprint Fee Was Received
We would like any suggestion to modify this list.

(5) How are the Status Levels defined ?
